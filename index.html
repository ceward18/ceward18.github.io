About Me

Welcome! I'm a PhD candidate in the Department of Biotatistics at the University of Iowa. My current research focuses on creating more realistic models of infectious diseases to better estimate disease transmission and the effectiveness of interventions. My collaborative research has spanned a wide range of disciplines, with recent work using machine learning and radiomics data for tumor classification and

I hold a strong passion for teaching introductory biostatistics and effective pedagogical methods. This led to applying and receiving funding for the creation of an Open Educational Resource (OER)to be used in an introductory biostatistics or statistics course. The OER is currently under construction, but our goal is to combine textual explanations and embedded simulation-based Shiny applications to provide students with an engaging and interactive learning resource which offers an intuitive illustration of the core statistical concepts.

I enjoy spending time with my family, baking, and all things volleyball-related.
